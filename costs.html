Estimating the cost of long term absence

Direct Costs
Continued Salary Payments:
During the initial period of sick leave, the employer may continue to pay the salary. This duration and amount can vary depending on company policy and local labor laws.
Medical and Healthcare Costs: If the company offers health insurance that covers mental health services, they might bear higher premiums or specific costs related to the employee's treatment for burnout.
Indirect Costs Replacement Costs:
Hiring a temporary worker or paying overtime to other employees to cover the duties of the absentee can be costly.
Lost Productivity:
The absent employee's work either slows down or must be redistributed among other team members, which can reduce overall productivity.
Decreased Morale:
The absence of a team member might affect the morale of the team, potentially reducing productivity further and increasing the risk of more health-related absences among other employees.
Administrative Costs:
Managing the absence, including adjustments to project timelines or redistributing responsibilities, involves administrative time and effort.
Reintegration Costs Gradual Return to Work: When the employee returns, they might start with reduced hours or lighter duties, which can extend the period of reduced productivity.
Support and Accommodation: Additional resources might be required to support the returning employee, such as counseling or changes to their work environment to prevent future burnout.

Example Calculation Let's assume an employee earns an annual salary of €30,000, and the company supports their salary fully during a six-month absence. The direct cost in terms of salary would be about €15,000. Adding potential increased healthcare costs, let’s say €500. For indirect costs, if we assume the productivity loss and the cost of hiring temporary help or overtime pay equals an additional 20% of their salary over this period, that would add another €3,000. Total Estimated Cost: Direct Costs: €15,500 (salary and increased healthcare costs) Indirect Costs: €3,000 (productivity loss, replacement costs) Total: €18,500 This estimate is simplistic and would vary significantly based on specific circumstances, such as the employee's role, the impact on ongoing projects, and the company's policies on handling long-term absences. Additionally, investing in preventive measures and a supportive work environment can reduce these costs significantly by lowering the incidence of burnout.
